## 1. Defense Tech & Platforms

- **[NEW 06-22] US accelerates F-47 in explicit response to J-36/J-50 [SIGNIFICANT].** The USAF's FY27 request jumps ~65% to **$5.03B** for the Boeing F-47, with officials citing fears China's two parallel sixth-gen programs could erode US air dominance in the 2030s. China's J-36 (Chengdu, land-based air-superiority) and J-50 (Shenyang, carrier-capable) have been in increasingly intensive flight testing since Dec 2024; operational capability projected mid-2030s. *Implication: a maturing PLAAF/PLAN sixth-gen track, now forcing a visible US budget reaction, widens the long-term air-power balance India must price in.* (Army Recognition, 19FortyFive, migflug — June 2026)
- **Sail-less ~120 m "seabed-warfare" submarine at Jiangnan (carried) [SIGNIFICANT].** Satellite imagery (~June 1) shows a ~120 m boat with no conventional sail and an X-form rudder — read as optimized for quiet, long-endurance operations; two new sub designs in one year suggests a dual-platform undersea strategy. *Implication: a harder-to-detect PLAN undersea capability that could extend into the IOR — a fresh ASW problem for the Indian Navy.* (TWZ, Zona Militar)
- **Fujian (Type 003) → FOC targeted end-2026 (context).** The 80,000-ton EMALS carrier (commissioned Nov 2025) is the first to launch a 5th-gen fighter (J-35) by catapult; extended Western Pacific deployments expected this year. *Implication: a third, far more capable carrier raises the ceiling for future IOR deployments.* (Global Times, USNI [403])

## 🇮🇳 India Watch — Net Assessment

The cycle is owned by **Wang Yi's arrival in New Delhi (June 22–23)** — the year's highest India–China touchpoint — now carrying a *confirmed* deliverable: **China resuming rare-earth magnet exports to India**, a calibrated carrot that eases real pressure on Indian automakers while underscoring the dependency. Crucially, the thaw runs *alongside*, not instead of, pressure: the named **Mustang Panda campaign against Indian banks**, the unresolved **Brahmaputra mega-dam**, and the tightening **China–Pakistan** track are the counterweights. On hard power, the signals are incremental but cumulative — **J-36/J-50 forcing a visible US F-47 budget reaction**, the **sail-less seabed-warfare submarine**, dual **Qianfan/Guowang** LEO mass, **DeepSeek V4 on Ascend**, and **quantum moving into industrial application** — each a reminder China's capability build and Global-South positioning continue regardless of the Delhi mood music. Externally, the **June 17 SCS collision** keeps a live MDT-trigger risk on the board India must factor into its Indo-Pacific posture.
