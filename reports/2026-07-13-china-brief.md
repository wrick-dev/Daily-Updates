## 🇮🇳 India Watch — Net Assessment

The **China-Russia "Joint Sea-2026" sea phase closed on schedule July 11**, with the **declared Pacific joint patrol now the pending watch item** — the blue-water template that most directly informs India's read of a future Chinese IOR standing presence.

The day's one unambiguous escalation in *capability* is technological, not operational: the **Long March 10B's July-10 maiden flight recovered an orbital booster for the first time** — a global-first net-catch sea recovery that makes China the second nation after the US to reland an orbital-class stage, and directly validates the reusability tech under the **2030 crewed-lunar program** and the cheap-heavy-lift trajectory that underwrites China's ~28,000-satellite LEO buildout. Beneath it the strategic ledger is unchanged and reinforced: the **JL-3/Type-094 sea-leg demonstration** remains the window's highest-consequence platform signal (CSIS confirming the July-6 shot impacted **inside the South Pacific Nuclear Free Zone** with hours' notice, and pressing a launch-notification agreement; Indian analysts stressing a more secure Pacific SSBN bastion **frees PLAN SSNs for the IOR**); the **Jiu Tian mothership's first operational mission remains overdue and unconfirmed (~13+ days past window)**; the Chinese AI stack is **both consolidating on Ascend and hedging toward DeepSeek's own inference silicon**, with **WAIC-2026 (July 17–20)** now the next fixed marker (Atlas 950 SuperPoD's first physical display); and the **PLA purge is read as widening**, hollowing the CMC to ~2 effective members and reaching the PLAAF, Western Theater Command and Cyberspace Force — a cautious near-term LAC positive, but Xi is **restaffing toward 2027, not retreating**.
