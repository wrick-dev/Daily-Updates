## 1. Defense Tech & Platforms

## 🇮🇳 India Watch — Net Assessment

The cycle is defined by the **eve of Wang Yi's 22–23 June New Delhi visit** — the year's highest India–China touchpoint — and Beijing is deliberately setting a warm table: Ambassador Xu's cooperative pre-visit messaging and the **start of rare-earth magnet license clearances for India** are calibrated carrots, while the BRICS NSA agenda (counter-terror + ICT security, "non-traditional threats") gives Delhi a platform to press its own priorities. But the thaw is bounded and runs *alongside*, not instead of, pressure: a fresh, named **Mustang Panda campaign against Indian banks** (and standing China-origin tooling like SyncFuture), the unresolved **Brahmaputra mega-dam**, the lack of any formal SR boundary round this visit, and a tightening **China–Pakistan** track are the counterweights. On the hard-power side the new signals are incremental but cumulative — **J-36 envelope expansion**, the **sail-less seabed-warfare submarine**, continued **Guowang/Qianfan** LEO mass, the **silicon-28 quantum** rung, and the **WAICO** Global-South AI-governance play — each a reminder that China's capability build and Global-South positioning continue regardless of the Delhi mood music. The genuinely strategic items remain material and informational; the genuinely India-relevant *event* is tomorrow's arrival in New Delhi.
